The Problem

If you cut in CapCut and finish in Premiere, you've hit this wall.

The export-and-flatten trap

You export your CapCut rough cut as MP4, drop it into Premiere, and now you have one clip. Every edit is baked in. Need to fix one cut? Back to CapCut. Re-export. Lose your Premiere work.

The rebuild-from-scratch tax

Open Premiere, use your CapCut export as a "reference," manually rebuild every cut. 2-4 hours per project. You're copying your own work.

The walled garden problem

Logic exports stems to Pro Tools. Figma exports to Sketch. Notion exports to Markdown. CapCut exports to nothing — and Adobe has no interest in importing it.

Why I'm building this

I've been editing professionally for 20 years. Documentaries, YouTube content for creators you've probably watched, corporate work — I've cut all of it.

Two years ago I started using CapCut for rough cuts. The speed is undeniable. But every time I needed Premiere-grade color or audio for client delivery, I'd hit the same wall: rebuild the entire edit. From scratch. Manually.

I waited for someone to fix it. Adobe wouldn't. ByteDance wouldn't. So in 2026, I started building CutSmith myself.
